Introduction

Knitted fabrics have a variety of end-uses, such as dress wear, bed linen, upholstery, carpets, etc. Knitting has developed into an important method of fabric manufacture and competes with woven fabrics for new shares in the market. Woven fabric is made by interlacing two sets of yarns, warp and weft, whereas knitted fabrics are formed by interconnecting loops from a single set of yarns. Depending on whether the yarn used for loop formation is lying length-wise or cross-wise, fabrics can be distinguished between warp (length-wise) and weft (cross-wise) knitted fabrics.

1.2 Basics of Loop Formation


In weft knitting, loops are formed and interlocked by hooked needles (bearded needles). As yarn is laid, sinkers move forward, bending the yarn to form loops. The structural components of these loops are known as the sinker loop and the needle loop. The figure illustrates the entire loop formation process.

The Needle


For the formation of loops, different knitting elements are required. The most important of these is the needle. There are three types of commonly used needles for knitting:

  • Latch Needles
